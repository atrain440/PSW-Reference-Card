Pattern Shift Wheel — Reference Card
The Pattern Shift Wheel maps eight inherited patterns across three domains. This card is your field guide to what each one looks like, what belief is underneath it, and what becomes possible when it shifts. Use it alongside the full Wheel visual.

What becomes possible
Gatekeeping
Controlling who has access to information, resources, or decisions.
Hoarding
A belief that sharing power or information means losing it.
Shared Decision-Making
Authority is distributed. Decisions made closest to the work.
Micromanagement
Leaders in the weeds of every detail, leaving little room for autonomy.
Distrust
A belief that control is the only way to ensure quality.
Shared Ownership
Real accountability for outcomes. Managers define success, then step back.
Practice · How we work
What you see
What's underneath
What becomes possible
Hustle Culture
Overwork normalized and rewarded. Busyness as a badge of honor.
Urgency Chasing
Busyness has become an identity, not just a workload.
Sustainable Pacing
Work designed around what people can sustain. Rest is part of the work.
Blame Culture
When things go wrong, focus lands on who's at fault, not what to learn.
Perfectionism
Mistakes feel like verdicts rather than data.
Learning Orientation
Accountability is about understanding, not punishment. Mistakes become information.
Analysis Paralysis
Decisions stalling in endless review. Nothing moves without certainty.
Certainty Seeking
A belief that moving before you have all the information is reckless.
Unlearning Orientation
Not-knowing is a starting point, not a stopping point. Learn from what happens.
Peacekeeping
Hard conversations avoided to maintain harmony. Conflict suppressed, not resolved.
People Pleasing
A belief that keeping the peace is safer than telling the truth.
Psychological Safety
People say the true thing, not the comfortable thing. Tension surfaces early.
Presence · How we connect
What you see
What's underneath
What becomes possible
Transactional Culture
Relationships purely task-based. People as means to an end.
Masking
Performing professionalism instead of bringing your full self.
Authentic Presence
Relationships have texture. Genuine investment in the humans doing the work.
Siloed Work
Teams operating in isolation. Information not flowing across the organization.
Individualism
Knowledge hoarded because your value feels tied to what only you can do.
Cross-team Collaboration
Teams share context, resources, and credit. Contributing beyond your department becomes normal.
